Elbaite

Na(Li,Al)3Al6(BO3)3Si6O18(OH)4

These samples of elbaite are on display in the Smithsonian Museum of Natural History. Elbaite is assigned the general formula Na(Li,Al)3Al6(BO3)3-
Si6O18(OH)4. This sample is from Brazil and is described as elbaite with albite. It is a silicate which has lithium content, lithium being a rather rare element. It also contains boron This sample is about 10cm across.

Elbaite is named after the area of the Island of Elba, Italy.

This magnificent sample of elbaite with albite and quartz is from the Tourmaline Queen mine, San Diego County, California. It is about 25 cm wide. As the elbaite crystals grew, the growth solution changed from manganese-rich to iron-rich, creating a blue top on each "candle" (This sample is nicknamed "Candelabra").

The first American tourmalines (elbaite) were discovered and mined in Maine. Maine has been a source for green elbaite. Currently the major U.S. source of elbaite, especially pink elbaite, is San Diego County, California.
