Determinant Evaluation Example

For a determinant of order three the evaluation rule is

Take the elements of the top row and multiply them times the determinant of their cofactors. The cofactor is the array left when the row and column of the given top row element is eliminated. The evaluation of the determinant of the cofactor follows the same pattern until the cofactor has dimension two. At that point, it's value is the difference of the diagonal products.
