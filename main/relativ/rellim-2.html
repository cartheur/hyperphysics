Threshold for Relativistic Effects

At what energies is a particle relativistic? It is convenient to know ranges of energies at which non-relativistic approximations are good enough. The energies at which non-relativistic expressions for mechanical quantities of electrons and protons are in error by 1% is a reasonable threshold for relativistic treatments.

The relativistic momentum

p = mv = gm0v

departs by 1% from the non-relativistic expression when g = 1.01 . This occurs for b = 0.14 or v = 0.14 c. The kinetic energy at this speed is (g - 1)m0c2 = 0.01m0c2. The 1% threshold is then 5.11 keV for electrons and 9.38 MeV for protons. For alpha particles, the 1% threshold for momentum is at 37.27 MeV. Applied to radioactive decay energies of around 1-10 MeV, this suggests that essentially all beta decay electrons are relativistic, but no alpha particles are relativistic.
