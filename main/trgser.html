Trig Function Series

Applications of Trig Series

One of the most important applications of trigonometric series is for situations involving very small angles. For such angles, the trigonmetric functions can be approximated by the first term in their series. This gives the useful small angle approximations:

Examples of the use of the small angle approximation are in the calculation of the period of a simple pendulum, and the calculation of the intensity minima in single slit diffraction. This approximation is used in most of the common expressions of geometrical optics which are built on the concept of surface power for lenses.
