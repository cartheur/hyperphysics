#2 Calculate strong force coupling constant at 1 TeV
The coupling constant is given by alpha=12*pi/((33-2*nf)*ln(E^2/lambda^2))
where nf = number of quark flavors = 6 and lambda is taken as 0.2 GeV
The value of the coupling constant at different energies is then
EE(MeV) alpha(strong)
1MeV 1 -0.169411879
100 -1.294960041
1GeV 1000 0.557708933
100000 0.144433549
1TeV 1000000 0.105386585

#9 Decays of the top quark
The expected decays will produce a bottom quark and a W+, so you look as what a W+ will produce
The possibilities include positron and electron neutrino, muon+ and muon neutrino, tau+ and tau neutrino
It can also decay into quarks like and up and an antidown, a charm and an antistrange, so mesons can be produced.
One of the channels above produces a prompt muon, but the decay of the bottom quark is likely to produce
muons from all of the decay schemes.
