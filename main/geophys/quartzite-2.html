Quartzite

Quartzite is a metamorphic rock form characterized by interlocking quartz grains from quartz sandstone.

This example of quartzite is from Warm Springs, Georgia where a stressed formation produces it along with schist and gneiss, two other metamorphic rocks. The formation produces a large warm spring.
