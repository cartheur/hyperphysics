Pyrite

Fe2S

Pyrite is Fe2S, an important example of a sulfide mineral. Often known as "fool's gold", it has a shiny metallic gold color. All the samples shown here are on display in the Smithsonican Museum of Natural History. This sample has a maximum width of about 15 cm and is from Navajun, La Rioja, Spain. It is a completely natural formation that shows extraordinarily precise cubic structure. This cubic geometery mirrors the internal cubic arrangement of the iron and sulfur atoms.

This sample is about 5 cm wide and is from Potosi', Brazil.

This compares the cubic pyrite sample with a goethite sample which formed after pyrite. The pyrite sample at right is from Ambassaguas mine, Amejun, La Rioja, Spain. It is about 15 cm wide.

The pyrite samples at right are from Amax mine, Boss, Missouri. The tallest column is about 25 cm high.

This sample is about 15cm high.

Examples of the different formations of pyrite.

The sample at right above is about 15cm wide.

Pyrite is a signifant mineral source for sulfur. It is not used as an ore for iron because of the difficulty of separating the iron from the sulfur.

The sample at right is pyrite forming with calcite. It is about 12 cm wide and is from Franklin, New Jersey.
