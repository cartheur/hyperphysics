Fowles and Cassiday give as example data for a satellite launch a low orbit velocity of about 8 km/s, an initial velocity of about 0.5 km/s from the rotation of the earth near the equator, and an exhaust velocity of 2.5 km/s. You could use this data above to confirm that you could get only about 5 percent of the total initial mass into orbit. They suggest that effective ejection speeds for most rocket fuels are on the order of 2 to 4 km/s.
